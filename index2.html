Overfeeding


What is Overfeeding ?

Such statements indicate a continuing global problem: over-consumption of food, leading to a lot of health and environmental problems. At the heart of this crisis lay mechanisms of food overproduction, overconsumption, wastes of food particularly among those items that are calorie, fat, sugar and/or processed ingredient-rich. Here's the manifestation and probable impacts it could have.

Health Consequences

Obesity: By feeding, the first major contributor to global increasing obesity rates. Obesity is rising alarmingly in many countries; hence it is evident that consuming more rather than required calories leads to being overweight and makes one more prone to chronic conditions such as heart diseases, diabetes, hypertension, etc., as well as some types of cancers.

Environmental Impacts

Food wastage: A large proportion of the food produced around the world is wasted, not only through excessive production but also in spoilage and disposal. This waste generates social as well as environmental problems like greenhouse gas emissions (because decomposing food in landfills produces methane-a very potent greenhouse gas) and wastes resources as precious as water and energy which are applied during food production.

Economic and Social Issues

From all over the world, increasing obesity and chronic diseases put an amazing burden on healthcare systems due to the cost of treatment that patients with heart problems and diabetes incur for the management of these highly expensive conditions.
