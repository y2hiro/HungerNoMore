Modern Solutions Towards World Hunger


Causes of World Hunger?

Globally, inflation and increased cost of living are a driving cause of world hunger, according to Reliefweb 8.5 million in Haiti are estimated to have insufficient food consumption as of March 2024. Furthermore, the continuous climate crisis is a leading cause of global hunger. In the past decade, 1.7 billion people have been affected by extreme weather and climate-related disasters according to the World Food Programme.

What Can We Do to Solve World Hunger?


On an individual level, one can start a planetary health diet. This diet focuses on the idea of our consumption being beneficial to our environment. To begin, it's crucial to minimize the consumption of all animal-based food sources, mainly processed food and red meats, while giving more attention to beans, nuts, seeds, and vegetables. On a global level, we should aim to eliminate food waste. 1/5 of food produced for human consumption is lost and 60% of food waste happens at the household level. We can begin by storing food correctly which can assist in prolonging the shelf life of food and reduce its deterioration. Utilizing leftovers is an easy step to start with!

Furthermore, implementing a school feeding system which gives children free or subsidized meals while they attend school, providing supply for these programs can come from urban agriculture. Urban agriculture is a more recent concept to combat agriculture but by its design, it can help cities to become more resilient; other benefits include density and redevelopment of empty areas.
